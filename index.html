We present a novel neural rendering approach for synthesizing photo-realistic novel views of dynamic scenes. Our method addresses the fundamental challenge of maintaining temporal consistency while achieving high visual fidelity.

Unlike prior work that treats each timestep independently or relies on complex motion estimation pipelines, we introduce a temporal-aware neural radiance field that jointly optimizes appearance and motion in a unified framework.

Our key innovation lies in the deformation field formulation, which models scene dynamics as continuous functions over space and time. This enables smooth interpolation between timesteps and robust generalization to unseen viewpoints.

Extensive experiments on standard benchmarks demonstrate that our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple metrics including PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS, while being significantly faster than competing methods.

Methodology

How It
Works

Our pipeline consists of four key stages that work together to achieve high-quality dynamic scene rendering.

01

Scene Encoding

Multi-view RGB images are encoded into a compact latent representation using our specialized encoder network.

02

Deformation Field

A learned deformation field maps canonical coordinates to deformed space at each timestep, capturing motion dynamics.

03

Neural Radiance

The neural radiance field predicts density and color at any point in the deformed space using MLP networks.

04

Volume Rendering

Differentiable volume rendering produces final images with physically-based lighting and realistic materials.
